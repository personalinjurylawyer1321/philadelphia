Common Causes of Truck Accidents in Philadelphia

Philadelphia's busy highways, including I-95, I-76, and I-476, see thousands of commercial vehicles daily. Common causes of truck accidents in our area include:

  • Driver fatigue and Hours of Service violations
  • Distracted driving and cell phone use
  • Speeding and aggressive driving
  • Improper loading and cargo securement
  • Mechanical failures and inadequate maintenance
  • Driving under the influence of drugs or alcohol
  • Weather-related accidents and poor visibility
  • Construction zone accidents

Types of Compensation Available

Truck accident victims in Pennsylvania may be entitled to various types of compensation, including:

  • Medical Expenses: Current and future medical bills, rehabilitation costs, and ongoing care
  • Lost Wages: Income lost due to inability to work, including future earning capacity
  • Pain and Suffering: Compensation for physical pain and emotional distress
  • Property Damage: Vehicle repair or replacement costs
  • Disability and Disfigurement: Compensation for permanent injuries and scarring
  • Wrongful Death: Compensation for families who have lost a loved one in a truck accident

Pennsylvania Truck Accident Laws and Deadlines

Pennsylvania has specific laws governing truck accident cases. The statute of limitations for personal injury claims is generally two years from the date of the accident. However, it's crucial to contact an attorney immediately because:

  • Evidence can be lost or destroyed
  • Witness memories fade over time
  • Trucking companies have teams of investigators working immediately after accidents
  • Electronic logging device data may be overwritten
  • Insurance companies begin building their defense immediately

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to hire a truck accident lawyer?
We work on a contingency fee basis, which means you pay no attorney fees unless we win your case. We offer free consultations to evaluate your truck accident claim and explain your legal options.
How long do I have to file a truck accident lawsuit in Pennsylvania?
In Pennsylvania, you generally have two years from the date of the truck accident to file a personal injury lawsuit. However, it's important to contact an attorney immediately to preserve evidence and protect your rights.
What makes truck accident cases different from car accident cases?
Truck accident cases are more complex due to federal regulations, multiple insurance policies, corporate defendants, and typically more severe injuries. They require specialized knowledge of FMCSA regulations and commercial vehicle operations.
How much compensation can I receive for my truck accident?
Compensation varies based on the severity of injuries, medical expenses, lost wages, and other factors. Our clients have received settlements and verdicts ranging from hundreds of thousands to over $2 million.
